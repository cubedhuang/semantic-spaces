_kama_ refers to arrival. Toki Pona draws a conceptual metaphor between arrival at a _location_ and arrival at a _state_.

:::ex
tok: mi **kama** lon tomo.
en: I've **arrived** at the house.
:::

Just as I can _kama_ into a room, I can also _kama_ being red, or _kama_ eating my food. In this sense, _kama_ refers to _becoming_ a certain way or _beginning_ an action.

:::ex
tok: ona li **kama** pona la mi kepeken.
en: I'll use it once it **becomes** good.
:::

:::ex
tok: mi **kama** lape.
en: I **fell** asleep.
:::

Beginning to do something and becoming a certain way are two sides of the same coin. English uses "begin" when the state is a verb and "become" when the state is an adjective. In Toki Pona, these are both described with _kama_.
