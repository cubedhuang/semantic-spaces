_wile_ is about desires and requirements. With an animate subject, a _wile_ is typically a desire. 

:::ex
tok: mi **wile** e moku suwi.
en: I want sweets.
:::

With inanimate subjects, _wile_ typically becomes a requirement.

:::ex
tok: pali ni li **wile** e tenpo mute.
en: This task **requires** a lot of time.
:::

_wile_ only translates well as 'need' when the need and the desire are associated with each other. For instance, I _need_ water, and if I become thirsty, the need for water _becomes_ a desire for water, so _mi wile e telo_. If I need to work for money but dislike my job, it wouldn't be accurate to say _mi wile pali mani_.

When the need of a subject is not connected to a desire, speakers often change the subject of _wile_ to refer to something whose _wile_ lines up with the need.

:::ex
tok: sijelo mi li **wile** e lape.
en: My body **requires** sleep.
en: I need sleep.
:::

In other cases, _o_ is used instead.
