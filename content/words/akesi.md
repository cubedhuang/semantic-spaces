_akesi_ are creatures that are typically cold to the touch. They normally can survive on land, but many _akesi_ also spend a lot of time in water.

Some _akesi_ are slimy, like frogs and salamanders. Some _akesi_ are scaly, like snakes and alligators. The prototypical _akesi_ is around the size of an open hand, but they can also be super large, like dinosaurs and dragons, or super small, like newts and nano-chameleons.

Many _akesi_ stay close to the ground when moving rather than walking upright, and pretty much all _akesi_ lay eggs.

Like other animal words, _akesi_ is a [folk taxonomic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Folk_taxonomy) term, not a scientific one; tadpoles are often described as _kala_ that will become _akesi_ rather than _akesi_ outright.
