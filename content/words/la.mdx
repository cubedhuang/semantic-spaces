_la_ is a grammatical "context-marking" particle. But context includes a lot of different things!

## Nouns as context

A noun placed before _la_ indicates that that noun is somehow involved in the sentence. It could describe a location, a topic, or just serve to change the word order.

:::ex
tok: tomo suli **la** jan li toki open.
en: **In** the main room, the speaker gave the opening keynote.
:::

:::ex
tok: mi **la** waso sina li suwi nanpa wan.
en: **To** me, your budgie is the cutest.
:::

:::ex
tok: kili loje **la** jan Sopi li moku e ona ale.
en: **As for** the strawberries, Sophie ate them all.
:::

Notice that the first two situations, saying _lon tomo suli_ and _tawa mi_ at the end of the sentence would mean the same thing. Placing something before _la_ is very generic and may encompass the meaning of all prepositions.

## Descriptive adjectives

An adjective placed before _la_ describes the whole sentence.

:::ex
tok: **ken la** nasin ni li pona ala.
en: **Maybe** (lit. **it's possible that**) this method won't work.
:::

:::ex
tok: **pona la** mi sona e ni lon tenpo pini.
en: **Fortunately** (lit. **it's good that**) I already knew this.
:::

## Time and cause

A complete clause can be placed before _la_ as a reference to the time or cause of the main clause.

:::ex
tok: mi wawa lili tan pali **la** mi lape.
en: I'm tired from work, **so** I'm going to sleep.
:::

:::ex
tok: sina weka **la** mi pilin ike.
en: **When** you're away, I'm afraid.
:::

## Multiple _la_

Multiple _la_ may be used in sequence. In these situations, it functions like 'then' in English.

:::ex
tok: mi seli e telo\
**la** ona li kama kon li tawa lon lupa\
**la** ona li kama lete\
**la** sin la kon li kama telo li tawa anpa lon poki ante\
**la** telo sin la jaki ala li lon.
en: **When** I heat up a liquid,\
it becomes a gas and travels through a tube,\
**then** it cools down,\
and **then** it becomes liquid again and falls into another container,\
and **then** in the new liquid, there are no impurities.
:::

Because of this, many speakers will use _la_ at the beginning of a sentence as a "continuation" of a previous speaker's sentence.

:::ex
tok: mi wile lape a... / **la** o lape!
en: I wanna sleep... / **Then** go sleep!
:::
