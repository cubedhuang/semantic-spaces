_kasi_ refers to plants and things resembling plants. This includes trees, shrubs, herbs, cacti, vines, grasses, and more.

Some speakers will use _kasi_ to refer to the edible leaves and stems rather than _kili_, like spinach, lettuce, and asparagus.

For some speakers, _kasi_ includes fungi and mushrooms; they resemble plants in being immobile life forms that grow in many of the same conditions that plants do. To other speakers, fungi are vastly different from plants and don't belong in the same conceptual category; these speakers often use _soko_ to describe fungi instead.
